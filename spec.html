Microbiology

Microbiology is the branch of biology that focuses on the study of microorganisms, including bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protozoa. It encompasses their morphology, physiology, genetics, and ecology, exploring their interactions with other organisms and their environments. Microbiologists employ a range of techniques such as microscopy, culturing, and molecular biology to understand the fundamental roles microorganisms play in health, disease, agriculture, industry, and the environment.

ENDOCRINOLOGY COUNSULTANT ENDOCRINOLOGIST

specializes in diagnosing and managing disorders related to hormones and the endocrine system. They treat conditions such as diabetes, thyroid disorders, adrenal gland disorders, pituitary gland problems, and reproductive hormone imbalances. Cardiology

deals with disorders of the heart and circulatory system. Cardiologists diagnose and treat conditions such as coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias (irregular heartbeats), and congenital heart defects. We use various diagnostic techniques including electrocardiograms (ECGs), echocardiograms, and cardiac catheterization to assess heart function and determine appropriate treatment plans which may involve medications, lifestyle changes, or procedures such as angioplasty or heart surgery.

OPHTHALMOLOGY

deals with the anatomy, physiology, and diseases of the eye. Ophthalmologists are medical doctors who specialize in the diagnosis, treatment, and management of eye disorders and conditions affecting vision. They perform comprehensive eye examinations to assess visual acuity, evaluate the health of the eyes, and diagnose diseases such as glaucoma, cataracts, diabetic retinopathy, macular degeneration, and refractive errors like nearsightedness and farsightedness.
